Sharpness Scale

  

This Sharpness Scale is used as a way to define relative sharpness, especially for tools which are not needing to be “razor sharp”.

Please also note, these are for linear edges, not for pointed objects (e.g., needles).

General Guidelines

Rating

Definition

Measurement

Examples & Comments
0 Totally blunt
  • Flat edge
  • Edge ≥ 2mm (1/16”) wide
  • Practice Swords
1 Blunt
  • Rounded edge
  • Edge ≥ 5mm (3/16”) diameter

 
2 Dull
  • Rounded edge
  • Edge < 5mm (3/16”) diameter
  • Butter knife
3 Dirty sharp
  • Dull edge, coming to a “point”
  • Gardening tools for digging into dirt
  • Metalworking bench tools
4 Heavy duty sharp  
  • Metal lathe tools
  • Masonry drill bits
5 Generally sharp  

 
6 High speed sharp  
  • Drilling bits, other than masonry bits
  • Saw blades
  • Scissors
7 High volume sharp  
  • Woodturning tools
  • Kitchen knives
8 Razor sharp  
  • Razors
  • Woodworking bench tools
9 Surgical sharp  
  • Surgical scalpels
10 Sharpest physically possible
  • 1 atom wide at cutting edge
  • (nothing yet)
