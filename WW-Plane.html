Edge angles

Values shown in the table below are for the Cutting Angle and the Relief Angle. As the Bed Angle is based on the plane body you are using, these are not shown. Additionally, the Front Bevel and Back Bevel can be easily determined from the Cutting Angle and the Relief Angle. To calculate these angles, use the following formulas:

Back Bevel = Bed Angle - Relief Angle

Notes & Comments

Information regarding Grindstones

Grind Shape & Experience

The shape of the grind used is a call best made by the tool's use, based on their own experience. Additional notes are available on separate web pages for: Grind Profiles, and Micro / Secondary Bevels.

Flattening the Back

Flattening the back is really critical for the overall sharpness. There is a good video about that below.

Relief Angles

A relief angle helps keep the force behind the cutting edge aligned with the movement of the plane (i.e., more in alignment with the surface of the wood than tangential to it). This is especially true for bevel down plane blades, and is also true for low angle planes.

More Information

Books & Papers

  • The Complete Guide to Sharpening, by Leonard Lee (2003). This is an excellent reference, and is recommended for every sharpener's library.
  • “Cut Your Honing Time in Half: Freehand Technique is much faster than jigs and guides”, by Hendrik Varju in “Fine Woodworking”. July/August 2008. Hendrik noted a number of techniques, and also how hollow grinding is a “secret of success”.
  • The Handyman's Book, a book by Paul N. Hasluck. My copy is the 1903 edition and is quite useful.
  • U.S. War Department's TM 9-867 Maintenance and Care of Hand Tools. Setup of the plane for various uses is nicely done.
  • “New Breed of Grinding Wheels”, by Chris Gochnour in “Fine Woodworking”. May/June 2025. Chris tested the sharpening of plane blades and chisels, comparing the use of traditional grindstones (on a high speed grinder) vs. CBN wheels.
  • The Razor Edge Book of Sharpening, by John Juranitch (2000)
  • Sharpen This, by Christopher Schwarz (2022). This is unique as it was written by a woodworker from the reference of his own sharpening.
