Notes & Comments

Information regarding Grindstones
  • Drill Bit Sizes (Metric, Fractional, Letter, & Number)
  • Tap & Clearance Drill Sizes (Metric & Fractional)

  • If you pursue sharpening small bits (< 1/8 in / < 3.0 mm),
    • A pin vice like the Starrett 166C is recommended. Having the pin vice's handle being larger than the knurled area allows the Tormek DBS-22 to hold the pin vice without it shifting. (Yes, that is something I learned the hard way.)
    • You will need a good magnifying glass and good light. These are difficult to see with the naked eye (especially in poor light).
    • Care must be taken when grinding the secondary facet on bits this small. It is very easy to over grind this. Check after each pass on the grindstone.
    • Consider honing the primary grind with a high grit stone (e.g., Tormek SJ-250 Japanese Waterstone). There is no need to hone the secondary facet as it is merely to allow space for chip evacuation.

  • For tougher materials, a secondary point angle (SPA) is useful for reducing the stress on the outside cutting lip corners. (This is well outlined in Joseph Mazoff's document.) However, the Tormek DBS-22 Drill Bit Sharpening Attachment is not designed for a grinding SPAs, so it is not outlined here.

  • Mica Schmid posted a nice USB spacer jig which makes it easer and faster to set the USB relative to the grindstone, and it also helps ensure the stone is consistently flat relative to the USB.

  • The Tormek DBS-22 Drill Bit Sharpening Attachment is not designed for a 6-facet grind, so values are not shown for it. A modification to the DBS-22 was made by Janne for 6-facet grinding, and more information can be found on the Tormek Forum.
